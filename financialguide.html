TLDR Priority Waterfall

There is a general order you should follow with extra money:

  1. Build a $1,000 emergency fund
  2. Pay off high-interest debt (e.g., credit cards, payday loans, etc.)
  3. Contribute enough to your employer’s retirement plan to get the full match
  4. Build a 3–6 month emergency fund
  5. Pay off moderate interest debt (e.g., student loans, personal loans, etc.)
  6. Contribute the maximum to your tax-advantaged retirement accounts (TFSA, RRSP, etc.)
  7. Save for other goals (e.g., travel, house, car, etc.)
  8. Pay off low-interest debt (e.g., mortgage)
  9. Invest in taxable investment accounts

Why is Investing Important?

The most important rule of investing is to start early. Even if you are only able to contribute a small amount, compound interest will work in your favor. Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world, as Albert Einstein famously said.

Investing allows you to grow your wealth over time, outpacing inflation. Inflation reduces the purchasing power of money over time, so it's essential to invest in assets that provide a return higher than the inflation rate.
